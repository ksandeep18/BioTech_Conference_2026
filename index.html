About the Conference

Emerging Trends in Structural Biophysics (CETSB 26)

Structural biophysics is moving beyond static models of biomolecules toward understanding their structure, dynamics, and function together. Advances like cryo-EM, NMR spectroscopy, molecular dynamics, and AI-driven tools such as AlphaFold are enabling researchers to study molecular motions, complex assemblies, and functional mechanisms with unprecedented clarity.

New perspectives on allosteric regulation and energy landscapes reveal that protein function often emerges from conformational ensembles, not just single states. This shift is reshaping research, training, and therapeutic strategies. CETSB 26 provides a platform for researchers, educators, and students to engage with these cutting-edge ideas and methods.

National Institute of Technology Warangal

Established in 1959, NIT Warangal is among India’s leading technical institutions, known for its academic excellence, vibrant research culture, and strong industry linkages. The lush green campus hosts advanced labs, modern infrastructure, and a diverse student community. The institute is just 2 km from Kazipet railway station and 190 km from Hyderabad airport.

Department of Biotechnology

Founded in 2006, the Department of Biotechnology at NIT Warangal offers B.Tech, M.Tech, and Ph.D. programs. With 20 faculty members and state-of-the-art labs, it focuses on both academic training and high-impact research. The department actively leads projects funded by DBT, DST, ICMR, and CSIR, and partners with industries to bridge the gap between science and real-world applications.

Visiting Places Near Warangal

Discover some of the must-visit attractions around Warangal during your trip.

Warangal Fort

Located 12 km from NIT Warangal, this fort reflects the grandeur of the Kakatiya dynasty. The iconic Kakatiya Kala Thoranam gateways are now Telangana’s state emblem.

Thousand Pillar Temple

A 12th-century marvel in Hanamkonda, renowned for its star-shaped layout, intricate carvings, and the majestic Nandi statue.

Ramappa Temple

A 13th-century UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for its lightweight “floating bricks” and exquisite stone sculptures.
